    Prediction of French elections retweets | INF554 Machine Learning and Deep Learning Sep. 2022 – Dec. 2022 #

    • Predicted the number of retweets regarding French elections 2022 based on dataset created by members of the DaSciM group, containing 1.2 million users and 62.6 million tweets that mention keywords relevant to the election.
    • Processed the text of the tweet with BERT, fine-tuned pre-trained model and crafted structures for the regression task
    • Analysed and extracted features in time series and Hashtags for sound inputs and compared the different results

    Research on multi-frequency mixed signal decomposition method | Bachelor Thesis, Sep. 2020 – Jun. 2021 #

    • Part of a state-level topic in China and supervised by Prof. Dongmei Fu, Bio-Inspired Computing and Intelligent Information Processing Lab, Beijing
    • Proposed a signal decomposition algorithm based on variational method which is scene adaptive on non-stationary signal and has higher accuracy compared with EMD, VMD in analysis of ECG signals and bearing signals
    • Analysed the signals and trends of corrosive atmospheric components as chlorine and sulfur around mainland China based on galvanic corrosion data collected by observation stations of State Grid Corporation of China (SGCC)
    • Programmed a signal decomposition and feature extraction toolbox (Copyright Registration: No.2021SR0872462)
    • Awarded the outstanding undergraduate thesis
